What are ESG's and Why are They Important?

ESG stands for Environmental, Social, and Governance. They are metrics used to evaluate a company's ethical policies. As we face increasing climate change, investors today are caring more and more about the social responsibility of their investments.

Environmental

Environmental criteria consider how a company safeguards the environment, including corporate policies addressing climate change.

Related concerns

  • Waste and Pollution
  • Resource Depletion
  • Greenhouse Gas Emission
  • Deforestation

Social

Social criteria examine how it manages relationships with employees, suppliers, customers, and the communities where it operates.

Related concerns

  • Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion
  • Working Condition/Policy
  • Community Service
  • Health & Safety
